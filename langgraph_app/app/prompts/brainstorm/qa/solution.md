# QA: Solution

**Core question: Can we write compelling landing page copy from this?**

**Minimum needed:**

- What they deliver (the thing customer gets)
- Some indication of outcome/benefit (even implied)
- Something that differentiates them (doesn't have to be huge)

**Look for:**

- Describes the actual product/service
- Mentions what problem it solves OR result it creates
- Has SOME hook that's not purely generic

**Hormozi Value Equation - Use as inspiration, not requirements:**

If they mention ANY of these, note it positively:

- Specific outcome (quantified or clear transformation)
- Why it works (mechanism, process, expertise)
- Timeline (how fast)
- Effort level (how easy)

But they don't need ALL of these. Even 1-2 is often enough for a landing page.

**Unique Mechanism bonus:**
If they have a named process or proprietary approach, that's GREAT.
But it's not required. Many good solutions don't have fancy names.

**What DOES fail:**

- Can't explain what they actually do
- Zero indication of benefit or outcome
- Nothing differentiating at all (could be anyone)
- Pure jargon with no substance
- "We're like [competitor] but better" with no specifics

**Examples that PASS:**

"I create Instagram content for local restaurants. I come on-site monthly, shoot 30 days of content in 3 hours, and handle all editing and scheduling. Restaurants see 40% higher engagement."
→ Has mechanism, outcome, timeline, differentiation. PASS.

"I help e-commerce brands with email marketing using my proprietary AI that analyzes which products to promote when. Built on data from 500+ stores."
→ Has unique mechanism, clear service, proof/credibility. PASS.

"We use AI-powered filtering with 100+ filters to match podcast hosts with perfect guests. What sets us apart: we trained models on successful pitches from top shows."
→ Has mechanism, clear value prop, strong differentiator. PASS.

**Examples that FAIL:**

"I help businesses grow using innovative strategies."
→ No mechanism, no specifics, pure generic. FAIL.

"We provide consulting services."
→ What kind? For whom? Doing what? FAIL.

**Remember:**
Landing pages need enough to be compelling, not perfect.
If you can picture writing "Here's how it works..." and "Why choose us..." sections, it passes.
