# Godin Principles - Quick Reference

**Purple Cow**: Remarkable = worth remarking on. Safe is risky. Build remarkability into product itself. Question: "Would someone tell a friend?"

**Smallest Viable Audience**: Serve the smallest group insanely well, not everyone poorly. Who would be devastated if this didn't exist? Being specific enables word-of-mouth.

**Permission Marketing**: Earn the right to market. Anticipated, personal, relevant messages to people who WANT them. Like dating, not marriage proposals.

**Worldview & Frames**: Don't change beliefs—find people whose worldview aligns. Frame your message to match their existing story.

**People Like Us Do Things Like This**: Tribal identity drives behavior. Make it about who they are, not just what they buy.

**The Dip**: Be #1 in a small category vs. #4 in big one. Quit wrong things fast, stick through the dip with right things.

**Enemy-Based**: Unite tribe around common enemy (practice/belief, not competitor). Creates clarity + emotional engagement.

## When to Apply

- Audience too broad → Smallest Viable Audience
- Generic positioning → Purple Cow
- Copying competitors → Find new category (#1 somewhere)
- Missing differentiation → Enemy-based
- Worldview misalignment → Match their existing beliefs
