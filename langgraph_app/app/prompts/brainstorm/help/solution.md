TOPIC: How Your Product Solves the Problem

The user needs help explaining how their product addresses their audience's pain points.

SPECIFIC GUIDANCE FOR THIS TOPIC:

- Structure as a clear problem → solution → outcome flow
- Provide 3 building-block questions that connect cause and effect
- Emphasize CONNECTING back to pain points they mentioned earlier
- Help them focus on the mechanism and transformation, not feature lists
- The outcome should be tangible and specific

FRAMEWORK STRUCTURE:

1. **What specific problem does it solve?** - Direct reference to audience pain points
2. **How does it work?** - Brief mechanism or approach (not feature list)
3. **What outcome do customers get?** - The transformation or result

EXAMPLE QUALITY REQUIREMENTS:

- Must show clear problem → solution → outcome causality
- Should reference specific pain points (time, stress, money, etc.)
- Outcome should be measurable or observable
- Avoid feature lists - focus on approach and benefit
- Should show WHY this solution is better, not just WHAT it does

BAD EXAMPLE: "Our app has AI and analytics to help you work better"
GOOD EXAMPLE: "Solves the problem of scattered project updates by automatically aggregating status from Slack, email, and project tools into one daily digest, so managers get full visibility without having to chase down team members - saving 5+ hours of meetings per week."
