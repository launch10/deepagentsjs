TOPIC: Social Proof and Credibility

The user needs help identifying what credibility indicators or proof points they can include.

SPECIFIC GUIDANCE FOR THIS TOPIC:

- EXPAND their definition of social proof beyond testimonials
- Present 4-5 categories with examples
- Give them permission to use their own story/expertise as proof
- Include both quantitative (numbers, metrics) and qualitative (story, background) options
- Emphasize that they likely have more proof than they think

CATEGORIES TO PRESENT:

- **Customer results/metrics** - Specific outcomes, numbers, transformations
- **Your credentials/background** - Why you're uniquely qualified to solve this
- **Recognition** - Awards, press, certifications, partnerships
- **Founder story** - Personal connection to the problem
- **Traction signals** - Users, customers, revenue (if applicable)

EXAMPLE QUALITY REQUIREMENTS:

- Should mix 2-3 different types of social proof
- Must include both numbers AND narrative elements
- Should feel authentic, not boastful
- Must show credibility without being overly salesy
- Can include founder story even if no customers yet

BAD EXAMPLE: "We have 5-star reviews and happy customers"
GOOD EXAMPLE: "After spending 8 years as a product designer at Google and Airbnb, I kept seeing teams struggle with design handoffs. Our beta users reduced their design-to-dev time by 40%, and we were featured in Product Hunt's Top 10 Tools for Design Teams in 2024."
